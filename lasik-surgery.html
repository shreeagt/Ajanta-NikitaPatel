What is LASIK Surgery?

LASIK (Laser-Assisted in Situ Keratomileusis) is a surgical procedure to correct refractive vision issues. However, after LASIK, some individuals might experience temporary symptoms during the healing process.

Dry Eyes

It's common to experience dryness, irritation, or a gritty sensation in the eyes after LASIK. This usually resolves as the eyes heal.

Glare and Halos

Some individuals may notice increased glare and halos around lights, especially at night, during the initial healing period

Blurry Vision

Vision might be slightly blurry immediately after the procedure, but it typically improves as the eyes heal.

Fluctuating Vision

Vision might fluctuate during the first few days or weeks after LASIK before stabilising

Light Sensitivity

The eyes might become more sensitive to light for a brief period after the procedure

Halos Around Lights

Similar to glare, halos around lights, especially at night, can be temporarily experienced.

Ghosting or Double Vision

Some individuals might temporarily experience ghosting or double vision, which usually resolves as healing progresses.

Mild Discomfort

While discomfort is minimal for most, some might experience mild discomfort or the sensation of a foreign object in the eye initially

It's important to note that these symptoms are usually transient and part of the healing process. If you're concerned about any of these symptoms or if they persist beyond the expected healing period, it's recommended to consult your eye care professional for guidance and reassurance.

Dos After LASIK Surgery

  • Follow Post-Op Instructions: Adhere strictly to the post-operative guidelines provided by your surgeon. These instructions are designed to promote healing and minimise complications.

  • Use Prescribed Eye Drops: Apply the prescribed eye drops as directed by your surgeon to prevent infection and aid in the healing process.

  • Protect Your Eyes: Wear the protective eyewear provided by your surgeon, especially during sleep, to prevent accidentally rubbing or touching your eyes.

  • Rest and Hydrate:Rest your eyes and give them time to heal. Adequate hydration supports overall healing and comfort.

  • Attend Follow-Up Appointments:Attend all scheduled follow-up appointments to monitor your healing progress and address any concerns.

  • Maintain Eye Hygiene: Keep your eyes clean and avoid makeup or creams around the eye area until your surgeon advises it's safe.

  • Shield from UV Light: Wear sunglasses when outdoors to protect your eyes from harsh sunlight and UV rays.

  • Stay Hygienic: Wash your hands thoroughly before touching your eyes or applying eye drops to prevent infection.

Don'ts After LASIK Surgery:

  • Avoid Rubbing Eyes: Refrain from rubbing, touching, or pressing on your eyes, as this can disrupt the healing process.

  • Limit Screen Time: Reduce screen time and avoid activities that strain your eyes, such as reading, watching TV, or using digital devices excessively.

  • Avoid Swimming and Hot Tubs: Steer clear of swimming pools, hot tubs, and other bodies of water for a few weeks to prevent infection.

  • Skip Strenuous Activities:Avoid strenuous exercise and activities that could lead to eye strain or accidental eye contact, such as contact sports.

  • No Eye Makeup:Avoid using eye makeup for at least a week or as advised by your surgeon to prevent debris from getting into your eyes.

  • Skip Using Taps and Sprays: Refrain from using tap water or sprays near your eyes, as they can introduce bacteria and irritate the healing tissues.

  • Avoid Rubbing Eyes: Refrain from rubbing your eyes, as it can worsen irritation or potential eye infections.

  • Avoid Driving Initially: It's best not to drive immediately after surgery. Wait until your vision stabilizes and your surgeon approves.

  • Skip DIY Remedies: Do not use any eye drops, medications, or remedies not prescribed by your surgeon.
